The Guide Group Client FAQ

What is The Guide Group? What services are offered?
The Guide Group is a trusted professional community of licensed, experienced guides and interpreters dedicated to delivering professional and personalized travel experiences. Our team ensures smooth arrangements and communication, and each experience is personally delivered by a licensed member of our professional community.
What is the contractual position? Who bears responsibility?
The group functions as a coordinator/contact point and is not a party to the contract. Service fulfillment, safety, legal compliance, and explanations are the responsibility of the assigned guide. Please contact our support if you have any questions.
How does the booking process work?
1. Communicate with our guide in advance or send your request → 2. Confirm conditions and quotation → 3. Pay 50% deposit online → 4. Guide assignment & itinerary confirmation → 5. Pay remaining 50% directly to guide on tour day → 6. Tour execution & feedback.
Where will I meet the guide?
Meeting points can be arranged at public locations such as the entrance of the first sightseeing spot, hotel lobby, café, shopping mall entrance, outside an Airbnb property, at a train station, or at a bus stop. For safety reasons, our guides cannot be asked to enter your room or any non-public/private space. The service fee will start from the actual meeting point with the guide.
Payment methods and remaining balance?
A 50% deposit is required online to confirm the booking. The remaining balance is paid directly to the guide on the tour day (cash, bank transfer, or major digital payments depending on the guide; confirm in advance).
Are receipts or invoices issued?
If you are a business client, the group can only issue a receipt for the booking handling fee (50% deposit portion). Receipts or invoices for the remaining 50% must be requested directly from the assigned guide.
For a receipt for the booking handling fee (50% deposit portion) in a corporate name, please contact booking@theguidegroup.com in advance.

What is the cancellation and refund policy?
  • More than 48 hours before the tour: full refund (6% processing fee deducted)
  • 24–48 hours before: 50% refund
  • Within 24 hours or no-show: no refund
Refunds are generally for the deposit portion. Transaction fees and exchange differences are borne by the client.
Can booking details or dates be changed?
Adjustments are possible depending on availability and cancellation policies. Contact us as early as possible. Additional fees may apply.
Meeting point and schedule rules? What if I'm late?
Please arrive 10 minutes before the scheduled start. The guide will wait up to 15 minutes. Beyond that, it will be treated as no-show and no refund will be issued. Tour time is calculated from the scheduled start time (late arrival does not reduce fees).
What is included or excluded in the fee?
Included: Guide service, itinerary coordination (communication by The Guide Group)
Excluded: Transportation, vehicle arrangements, meals, admission fees, insurance, personal expenses (unless specified). Necessary costs are clarified in the pre-tour estimate.
Overtime charges—how are they calculated?
Overtime requires prior agreement. Rates follow the "Overtime Add-on" in the package or previously provided rates. On-the-day extension requires confirmation with the guide; if not agreed, the tour ends at the scheduled time.
What happens in bad weather or force majeure?
Tours proceed in light rain. Typhoons, severe warnings, or major transportation disruptions may lead to rescheduling or refunds. The group is not liable for delays, cancellations, or extra costs due to force majeure.
Safety measures and insurance?
Guides prioritize safety, but clients are strongly recommended to have travel/medical insurance. Notify guides in advance of medical conditions or allergies. Requests involving illegal or dangerous acts will not be accepted.
Languages and interpretation scope?
Guides provide only the languages listed in their profile. Legal, medical, or technical interpretation is excluded. Professional interpreters can be arranged separately (additional fee).
Can children, elderly, or wheelchair users participate?
Yes. Routes, rest stops, and accessibility are considered. Wheelchair accessibility or special assistance is confirmed in advance. Escorts may be required.
Maximum group size?
Typically 1–10 people. Larger groups may require additional guides or vehicles (additional cost).
Hotel pickup or meeting arrangements?
Pick-up is possible at major hotels, stations, or airports. Timing and location are confirmed with the guide. Long-distance or airport pickups may incur extra charges.
Vehicle charter available?
We strongly recommend doing so. In fact, 65–80% of our clients book our private guide service together with a charter vehicle. Charter vehicles are provided directly by our licensed professional partner companies (separate fees apply). The guide, driver, and client will confirm the routes and safety together. Illegal stops or unsafe requests cannot be accepted.
Meal and allergy accommodations?
Provide allergies, dietary restrictions, or preferences in advance. Multiple restaurant options will be suggested considering hygiene and congestion. Peak times may involve waiting (some reservations may incur fees).
Is tipping required?
Tipping is optional in Japan. You may give as a gesture but are not obligated. Packages that include tips are clearly stated.
Can I take photos or videos?
Allowed in most places, respecting facility rules and others' privacy. Guides will advise where photography is prohibited. Notify in advance if anyone prefers no photos.
Can the itinerary be changed on the day?
Changes are possible within reason, but extra time or costs may apply. The guide will explain feasibility, effects, and fees on the spot, and changes will be made with agreement; otherwise, the original plan proceeds.
Shopping or commercial support?
Shopping accompaniment and interpretation support are possible. The guide will explain restrictions (duty-free, transport, hours, stock, etc.). No forced purchases, kickbacks, or rebate-driven guidance will be made. High-value purchases are at the client’s own discretion (advice can be provided).
Health issues or emergencies?
Safety is the top priority. In case of emergency, the guide will contact 119/110 and assist you to the nearest hospital or police box. Payment methods may vary depending on your insurance coverage. Please notify both the guide and the group promptly.
Confidentiality of client information?
Information is handled strictly for booking, operation, and after-support purposes. Storage and deletion follow group policy. No disclosure to third parties without consent, except when required by law. Please refer to the Privacy Policy for details.
Communication before and during the tour?
Communication is mainly via email, livechat, or online meeting (link provided as arranged). Please ensure you can always be reached for smooth coordination.
What if I get lost or cannot meet the group?
First, go to the last place where you and the guide met. Keep communication open so the guide can contact you. If public Wi-Fi is unreliable, prepare mobile internet access in advance. Please note that waiting and reconnection time is still counted as tour time.
What if I lose belongings during the tour?
Clients are responsible for their own belongings. Large luggage should be left in lockers or hotels. Guides may assist with lost-and-found reports or safe storage advice but are not liable for loss, theft, or damage.
Conduct and manners during the tour?
Please observe public manners, avoid discriminatory or aggressive behavior, and refrain from dangerous acts. The guide cannot accompany you if your actions violate laws or facility rules. For safety, the guide may issue instructions or stop the tour if necessary.
Can you reserve tickets for facilities or events?
Yes, if the facility allows (some may not or may charge fees). Ticket and reservation costs are borne by the client. Popular facilities may sell out early, so early consultation is recommended. Proxy purchases depend on facility regulations.
How is tour time calculated? Does travel time count?
Tour time is calculated as the actual time from the scheduled meeting to the end. Travel time by walking, public transport, or vehicle is included. Waiting, coordination, and breaks are also counted unless otherwise specified in advance.
Can tours be held in multiple languages?
Normally up to 1–2 languages only. Multi-language tours may slow efficiency, so additional guides may be recommended (extra fees). Language roles should be clarified in advance.
Is photography service (by the guide) included?
Basic snapshots with smartphones are possible. For professional photography (equipment, editing, etc.), a professional photographer can be arranged (extra fee). Facility rules on photography apply.
Can I request a specific guide?
Yes. An extra fee may apply. If your preferred guide is unavailable, a suitable alternative will be proposed. Final confirmation is after deposit payment (pre-hold is generally not possible).
Early morning or late-night tours?
Extra fees may apply for early morning or late-night hours due to safety and transport availability. Feasibility depends on guide schedule, area, and itinerary. Please consult in advance.
Non-sightseeing requests (business meetings, inspections)?
Possible. Costs depend on specialization and workload. Legal, medical, or technical interpretation is excluded or will require professional interpreters (extra fees). Confidentiality needs should be notified in advance.
Can minors join without parents?
Generally, parental accompaniment is required. For special cases (such as school arrangements), parental consent and emergency contacts are necessary. Solo minor participation may be declined for safety reasons.
Can I bring pets?
Depends on facility and transport rules. Carriers are often required, and some places prohibit entry. Pets may cause itinerary restrictions, so please consult in advance. The group and guides are not responsible for pet-related issues.
Can I post on SNS or review sites?
Yes, feedback is welcome. However, please avoid posting personal data, other clients’ faces, or content prohibited by facility rules. If unsure about photo posting, ask our guide (same applies for staff face photos).
What if there is a payment or refund dispute?
Please contact our group’s customer support team. Logs, receipts, and agreements will be reviewed with the guide. Final responsibility for service delivery and receipt lies with the guide. Disputes with credit card companies follow their rules.
Requests that violate laws or regulations?
Not possible. All laws, facility rules, and photography restrictions are strictly followed. Illegal requests will be refused, and the tour may be changed or cancelled (refund not guaranteed).
Can guides pay in advance for tickets or cash?
Normally not allowed. Advance-purchase tickets are handled via online payment links or official sites. In exceptional cases, if advance payment is made, a written agreement and receipt are required, and a handling fee may apply (case by case).
Tours outside the usual area?
Extra travel and transportation costs may apply for areas far from the guide’s base. For efficiency and safety, another guide may be recommended. Details are explained during quotation.
What if there is a serious complaint or dispute?
First, our group’s customer support team will handle it, confirming facts and coordinating. Please keep logs, itineraries, and agreements. Final responsibility rests with the assigned guide. Governing law and jurisdiction are Japanese law and courts.
Where can I check the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy?
Links to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy are included on the booking screen and in the booking confirmation email. We recommend saving the URL(s) for future reference. If you have any questions, please contact us.
