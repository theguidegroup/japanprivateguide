FAQ for The Guide Group Clients

What is The Guide Group? What services are offered?
The Guide Group is a trusted professional community of licensed guides and experienced interpreters dedicated to providing professional, personalized travel experiences. Our team ensures effective arrangements and communication, and each experience is delivered personally by a certified member of our professional community.

We aim to make travel more than just visiting destinations; it’s about connecting with cultures, creating lasting memories, and exploring the world with confidence and insight. By blending professional expertise with technological innovation, we ensure every journey is meaningful, accessible, and unforgettable.
Who is responsible under the contract?
The group acts as a coordinator/contact point and is not a party to the contract. Service delivery, safety, legal compliance, and explanations are the responsibility of the assigned guide. Contact our support team if you have any questions.
How does the booking process work?
1. Communicate with your guide in advance or send your request → 2. Confirm terms and quote → 3. Pay a 33% deposit online → 4. Guide assignment and itinerary confirmation → 5. Pay the remaining 67% directly to the guide on the tour day → 6. Enjoy the tour and provide feedback.
Where will I meet the guide?
The meeting point can be agreed upon in public places such as the entrance to the first attraction, hotel lobby, café, mall entrance, outside an Airbnb, train station, or bus stop. For safety reasons, guides cannot enter your room or private spaces. The service fee starts from the actual meeting point with the guide.
Payment methods and balance?
A 33% deposit is required online to confirm the booking. The remaining balance is paid directly to the guide on the tour day (cash, bank transfer, or major digital payment depending on the guide; confirm in advance).
Are receipts or invoices issued?
For receipts or invoices, please contact the assigned guide directly.
What is the cancellation and refund policy?
  • The 33% deposit paid to confirm the booking is generally non-refundable.
  • However, if the tour cannot be provided by the guide and no equivalent replacement can be arranged, the deposit will be refunded minus a 6% handling fee.
  • Please notify us as soon as possible if you need to cancel or reschedule.
Can I modify booking details or dates?
Modifications are possible based on availability and the cancellation policy. Contact us as soon as possible. Additional fees may apply.
Rules on meeting point and time? What if I am late?
Please arrive 10 minutes before the scheduled time. The guide will wait up to 15 minutes. After that, it is considered a no-show, and no refund will be issued. Tour time is calculated from the scheduled start time (late arrival does not reduce fees).
What is included or excluded in the fee?
Included: Guide service, itinerary coordination (communication via The Guide Group)
Excluded: Transportation, vehicle setup, meals, admission fees, insurance, personal expenses (unless specified). Necessary costs are clarified in the pre-tour quote.
How are overtime charges calculated?
Overtime requires prior agreement. Rates follow the “Overtime Add-on” in the package or rates provided in advance. Same-day extensions must be confirmed with the guide; if not agreed, the tour ends at the scheduled time.
What happens in case of bad weather or force majeure?
Tours proceed in light rain. Typhoons, severe alerts, or major transport disruptions may require rescheduling or refunds. The group is not responsible for delays, cancellations, or extra costs due to force majeure.
Safety measures and insurance?
Guides prioritize safety, but clients are strongly recommended to have travel/health insurance. Inform the guide in advance of any medical conditions or allergies. Illegal or dangerous requests are not accepted.
Languages and scope of interpretation?
Guides provide only the languages listed in their profile. Legal, medical, or technical interpretation is excluded. Professional interpreters can be arranged separately (additional cost).
Can children, seniors, or wheelchair users participate?
Yes. Routes, breaks, and accessibility are considered. Wheelchair access or special assistance must be confirmed in advance. Accompanying persons may be required.
Maximum group size?
Typically 1–10 people. Larger groups may require extra guides or vehicles (additional cost).
Hotel pickup or meeting arrangements?
Pickup at hotels, stations, or airports is possible. Times and locations are confirmed with the guide. Long-distance or airport pickups may incur extra costs.
Is vehicle rental available?
Highly recommended. 65–80% of our clients book a guide along with a private chartered vehicle. Vehicles are provided by our authorized partner companies (separate cost). Guide, driver, and client confirm routes and safety together. Illegal or unsafe stop requests cannot be accepted.
Meals and accommodations for allergies?
Provide allergies, dietary restrictions, or preferences in advance. Multiple dining options will be suggested considering hygiene and crowding. Peak periods may involve waiting; some bookings may incur extra costs.
Is tipping mandatory?
Tipping is optional in Japan. You may leave it as a token of appreciation but it is not required. Packages that include tips are clearly indicated.
Can I take photos or videos?
Allowed in most places, respecting facility rules and others’ privacy. Guides will indicate where photography is prohibited. Notify in advance if someone prefers not to be photographed.
Can the itinerary be changed during the day?
Changes are possible within reasonable limits, but may incur extra costs or time. The guide will explain feasibility, effects, and fees on the spot; modifications are made by agreement, otherwise the original plan proceeds.
Support for shopping or business interpretation?
Accompaniment for shopping and interpreter support is possible. The guide will explain restrictions (duty-free, transport, hours, availability, etc.). No mandatory purchases, kickbacks, or profit-driven guiding. High-value purchases are at client discretion (guidance may be provided).
Health issues or emergencies?
Safety is the priority. In emergencies, the guide will contact 119/110 and assist until the nearest hospital or police station. Payment methods may vary depending on insurance coverage. Communicate promptly with both guide and group.
Client data privacy?
Information is strictly managed for booking, operational, and post-service support purposes. Storage and deletion follow group policy. No disclosure to third parties without consent, except as required by law. See the Privacy Policy for details.
Communication before and during the tour?
Communication is mainly via email, live chat, or online meetings (links provided as agreed). Ensure you are always reachable for smooth coordination.
What if I get lost or miss the group?
First, go to the last point where you met the guide. Keep communication open so the guide can contact you. If public Wi-Fi is unstable, prepare mobile Internet access in advance. Waiting and reconnection time counts as tour time.
What if I lose items during the tour?
Clients are responsible for their own belongings. Large luggage should be left in storage or hotel. Guides can assist with lost item reports or safe storage advice but are not responsible for loss, theft, or damage.
Behavior and etiquette during the tour?
Please respect public norms, avoid discriminatory or aggressive behavior, and refrain from dangerous actions. The guide may refuse to accompany if your actions violate laws or facility rules. For safety, the guide may give instructions or stop the tour if needed.
Can you book tickets for facilities or events?
Yes, if the facility allows (some may not or may charge fees). Ticket costs and reservations are borne by the client. Popular facilities may sell out quickly, so early consultation is recommended. Delegated purchases depend on facility rules.
How is tour time calculated? Is travel time included?
Tour time is calculated from the scheduled meeting until the end. Walking, public transport, or vehicle travel time is included. Waiting, coordination, and breaks are also counted unless specified otherwise in advance.
Can tours be conducted in multiple languages?
Typically up to 1–2 languages. Multilingual tours may slow efficiency; additional guides may be recommended (extra cost). Language roles should be clarified in advance.
Is photography service (by guide) included?
Basic photos with smartphones are allowed. For professional photography (equipment, editing, etc.), a professional photographer can be arranged (extra cost). Facility photography rules apply.
Can I request a specific guide?
Yes. An extra fee may apply. If your preferred guide is unavailable, a suitable alternative will be proposed. Final confirmation occurs after deposit payment (usually cannot “reserve in advance” without deposit).
Early morning or late-night tours?
Extra costs may apply for night or early morning tours due to safety and transport availability. Feasibility depends on guide schedule, location, and itinerary. Consult in advance.
Non-tourist requests (meetings, inspections)?
Possible. Costs depend on specialization and workload. Legal, medical, or technical interpretation is excluded or requires professional interpreters (extra cost). Confidentiality needs must be communicated in advance.
Can minors participate without parents?
Parental accompaniment is generally required. For special cases (e.g., school arrangements), parental authorization and emergency contacts are necessary. Unaccompanied minor participation may be refused for safety reasons.
Can I bring pets?
Depends on facility and transport rules. Cages are often required, and some places may prohibit entry. Pets may limit the itinerary, so check in advance. The group and guides are not responsible for pet-related issues.
Can I post on social media or review sites?
Yes, feedback is welcome. However, avoid posting personal data, other clients’ faces, or content prohibited by facility rules. If unsure about posting photos, ask the guide (same applies to staff photos).
What happens in case of payment or refund dispute?
Contact the group support team. Logs, receipts, and agreements with the guide will be analyzed. Final responsibility for service and receipts rests with the guide. Credit card disputes follow card company rules.
Requests that violate laws or regulations?
Not allowed. All laws, facility rules, and photography bans are strictly followed. Illegal requests will be refused, and the tour may be modified or canceled (no refund guaranteed).
Can guides advance payments for tickets or cash?
Generally not allowed. Tickets purchased in advance are handled via online payment links or official sites. In exceptional cases, if advance payment is made, a written agreement and receipt are required, and a handling fee may apply (case by case).
Tours outside the usual area?
Additional charges may apply for travel and transportation to areas far from the guide's base. For efficiency and safety, another guide may be recommended. Details will be explained during the quote process.
What happens in the event of a serious complaint or dispute?
First, the group's support team will handle the issue by verifying the facts and coordinating. Keep logs, itineraries, and agreements. Final responsibility lies with the assigned guide. The applicable law and jurisdiction are Japanese.
Where can I consult the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy?
Links to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy are included on the booking screen and in the booking confirmation email. We recommend saving the URL(s) for future reference. If you have any questions, contact us.
