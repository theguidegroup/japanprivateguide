A Sacred Island and Shrine in Harmony

Originally constructed in the 6th century and redesigned in its current form in 1168 by the powerful warlord Taira no Kiyomori, Itsukushima Shrine was built to honor the daughters of the sea deity Susanoo-no-Mikoto. Because the island itself is considered sacred, the shrine was uniquely built on stilts over the sea, allowing worshippers to approach without setting foot on the hallowed ground.
